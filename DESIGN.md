# Design System: Gilt & Grove

## 1. Overview

**Creative North Star: "The Candlelit Bower"**

Gilt & Grove is built from a single atmospheric moment: stepping across the threshold of a night barn lit by candles. Wrought iron goes warm in firelight; eucalyptus hung from the rafters catches the glow; tablecloths of floral white float beneath bronze lanterns. The design holds that feeling — dark, regal, and wholly alive — before the ceremony has even begun. Every surface decision flows from this scene.

The visual system pairs two historical registers that belong together: insular Celtic knotwork (the woven permanence of interlace, the trefoil knot binding the names) set inside angular Art Deco framing (crisp rules, notched corners, sunburst halos). Neither element is decoration. The knotwork is structural — generated by an algorithm, not clip-art — and the Deco geometry is the armature that keeps it from becoming folk art. Together they read as formal and handcrafted at once, which is exactly what this wedding is.

The palette is committed: dark near-black grounds and eucalyptus greenery as the single lead, with bronze used only as the fine metallic tie. Motion is slow and graceful — no bounce, no overshoot. The type system holds three faces in extreme contrast: Celtic insular Uncial display for the couple's names, inscriptional Roman caps for every structural element, and a warm high-contrast serif for the prose that carries their voice.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warm near-black grounds — wrought iron and chairs at night; never pure tech-black, never light
- Eucalyptus greenery as the single lead color — the hung greenery of the barn
- Antique bronze as the fine metallic tie — the "&", divider knots, corner ornaments; never a dominant fill
- Celtic knotwork as structural, generated ornament — never botanical filigree or clip-art
- Angular Deco framing — near-sharp corners (3px default), crisp hairlines, sunburst halos
- Slow, graceful motion — `cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1)`, 160–700ms durations, no bounce
- Three-face type system in deliberate contrast — Celtic display / Roman caps / humanist serif

## 2. Colors: The Barn Palette

Antique, natural tones drawn from a candlelit night barn: warm near-black iron grounds, floral white blooms, eucalyptus greenery, and the glint of bronze.

### Primary

Greenery is the single lead color. It carries sections solo or paired with floral white; it appears in solid fills, hairlines, and the outer bands of the couple's signature foil gradient.

- **Eucalyptus — Lightest** (`#90A587` / `--green-300`): Lightest accent text — eyebrows, links, inline labels on dark grounds. Tuned for WCAG AA at 13px tracked-cap text on `--ink-800`.
- **Eucalyptus — Accent** (`#5F7456` / `--green-400`): Solid fill for the green button variant; `--accent-green` alias. More vivid than core for interactive elements.
- **Eucalyptus — Core** (`#47593F` / `--green-500`): The heart of the system. The `--accent` semantic alias; used in hairlines, the center band of the duo-sheen gradient, and structural greenery rules.
- **Deep Foliage** (`#22301E` / `--green-700`): Shadow end of greenery fills; text-selection background base.

**Signature gradients** (rendered via CSS `background` / `background-clip: text`; full CSS values in `.impeccable/design.json`):
- `--duo-sheen`: greenery ⟷ bronze ⟷ greenery — the couple's foil signature, for their names and the primary CTA
- `--gilt-sheen`: metallic bronze sweep — for the "&" ornament and small gilt labels
- `--duo-line`: green → bronze → green — for important frame/rule borders

### Neutral

- **Wrought Iron — Page Bg** (`#121110` / `--ink-800`): The page background. Warm near-black — a trace of brown warmth, never blue-black, never #000.
- **Wrought Iron — Deep** (`#0A0A08` / `--ink-900`): Deepest ground; text color on accent fills (`--text-on-accent`).
- **Wrought Iron — Surface** (`#1B1A17` / `--ink-700`): Raised surfaces; default input background.
- **Wrought Iron — Card** (`#262320` / `--ink-600`): Card and panel backgrounds.
- **Wrought Iron — Hover** (`#33302A` / `--ink-500`): Hover fill; hairline ground.
- **Floral White** (`#FBF7EE` / `--parchment-100`): Primary body text on dark grounds. Warm cream white — the tablecloth color. Never `#FFFFFF`.
- **Floral White — Soft** (`#C9BFAC` / `--parchment-400`): Secondary copy; `--text-soft`. Verify AA at small sizes before use.
- **Graphite** (`#B5AFA4` / `--graphite-200`): Muted text (`--text-muted`). AA-calibrated for `--ink-800`.
- **Graphite — Deep** (`#67625A` / `--graphite-400`): Input placeholder text. Clearly below body text in hierarchy.

### Metallic Accent (Bronze)

Bronze does not fill surfaces. It appears at junctions — the "&" between names, the diamond in a rule, the corner ornament in a frame, the glow halo on a focused button.

- **Champagne Bronze** (`#E6CB8E` / `--gold-300`): Bright highlight in gilt gradients; `--metal-strong` alias.
- **Antique Bronze — Core** (`#B18A44` / `--gold-500`): The workhorse metallic; `--metal` alias. Used in the rule diamond, `--gilt-sheen` midpoint, and focus ring accents.
- **Deep Bronze** (`#664C22` / `--gold-700`): Shadow end of the gilt sheen gradient.

### Functional

- **Error Red** (`#B4524A` / `--critical`): Sits entirely outside the brand palette. For form validation errors only — never decorative.

### Named Rules

**The Bronze Tie Rule.** Bronze and gold appear on the "&", divider knots, corner ornaments, and as glow halos in focus states. They are never a dominant surface fill, never a button background on their own, never a section background. Their rarity is what makes them feel precious.

**The Dark Ground Rule.** Every page surface uses `--ink-800` (#121110) or a darker ink step as its primary ground. Parchment appears only as a card variant on an otherwise dark page — never as a full-page background.

**The Hairline Rule.** Borders are always 1–2px and semi-transparent (16–30% opacity floral-white, 42% bronze, or 60% green). No solid opaque borders. The Deco geometry lives in the presence of the line, not its weight.

## 3. Typography

**Celtic Display: Uncial Antiqua** ('Uncial Antiqua', Cinzel, serif)
**Structural / Label: Cinzel** ('Cinzel', Times New Roman, serif)
**Body: Cormorant Garamond** ('Cormorant Garamond', Georgia, Times New Roman, serif)

**Character:** The three faces are in extreme contrast — Celtic insular hand, inscriptional Roman capitals, warm Renaissance humanist serif. They do not blend; each occupies its own register, its own moment. The combination reads as ancient, ceremonial, and deeply personal; nothing like a wedding template.

### Hierarchy

- **Celtic Display** (Uncial Antiqua, 400, `clamp(40px, 8.2vw, 72px)`, lh 1.04, ls 0.04em): Reserved exclusively for the couple's names and ceremonial role titles where their names appear. Apply `--duo-sheen` foil via `background-clip: text` for the hero and primary CTA; bare for monochromatic contexts.
- **Display** (Cinzel, 600, `clamp(40px, 8.2vw, 72px)`, lh 1.04, ls 0.04em): Page heroes and section introductions where the couple's names are not the subject. The carved Roman workhorse.
- **Headline** (Cinzel, 600, `clamp(34px, 6vw, 52px)`, lh 1.04, ls 0.04em): Primary content headings in `--parchment-100` on dark grounds.
- **Title** (Cinzel, 500, `clamp(28px, 4.4vw, 38px)`, lh 1.2): Sub-section headings; medium weight avoids competing with Headline.
- **Subheading** (Cormorant Garamond, 600, `clamp(22px, 3vw, 27px)`, lh 1.2): Warmer, more personal voice for card headings and story subheadings where Cinzel would feel too stiff.
- **Body** (Cormorant Garamond, 400, 18px floor, lh 1.55, ls 0.005em): All narrative prose and guest-facing copy. Italic carries emphasis — never bold. Line length capped at 65–75ch.
- **Label / Eyebrow** (Cinzel, 400, 13px, ls 0.34em, uppercase): Section kickers, form labels, nav items, UI metadata. Rendered in `--green-300` (#90A587) on dark grounds for WCAG AA. Wide tracking (0.34em) gives the inscriptional Roman caps their architectural weight.

### Named Rules

**The Three-Face Pact.** Uncial Antiqua for the couple's names only. Cinzel for everything structural — headings, labels, nav, eyebrows. Cormorant Garamond for everything spoken — body copy, captions, story prose. Mixing voice roles (a Cinzel body paragraph; an Uncial section heading) breaks the system immediately.

**The Italic Emphasis Rule.** In Cormorant body copy, emphasis is always `<em>` (italic), never `<strong>` (bold). The face's high-contrast hairlines make italic the natural emphasis register; bold reads as size change, not stress.

**The No-Script Rule.** No cursive or handwritten typefaces appear anywhere in this system. Romance is carried by knotwork, metal, and type — never by letterform affectation.

## 4. Elevation

The system is flat by default. Surfaces are differentiated by the ink ramp — `--ink-700` raised above the `--ink-800` page background, `--ink-600` for cards — not by drop shadows. Shadows appear only as state responses (hover, active, promoted panels) or as the warm gilt glow on emphasis elements and focus states.

Two distinct shadow moods: **ambient depth** (deep near-black drops for structural lift) and **gilt glow** (warm bronze ring + amber halo for interactive emphasis and focus).

### Shadow Vocabulary

- **`--shadow-sm`** (`0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.45)`): Subtle lift for chips and small elements.
- **`--shadow-md`** (`0 6px 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.5)`): Elevated cards at rest; sticky nav on mobile.
- **`--shadow-lg`** (`0 18px 48px rgba(0,0,0,0.58)`): Maximum lift — hovered interactive cards, open modal/flyout panels.
- **`--glow-gold`** (`0 0 0 1px rgba(177,138,68,0.35), 0 8px 30px rgba(177,138,68,0.12)`): Bronze emphasis halo. Duo and gold buttons on hover; primary CTA card at rest for warmth.
- **`--glow-gold-strong`** (`0 0 0 1px rgba(230,203,142,0.6), 0 10px 36px rgba(177,138,68,0.22)`): Intensified focus indicator for inputs and form controls.
- **`--focus-ring`** (`rgba(144,165,135,0.55)`): The sage halo used in the standard button focus ring (`0 0 0 2px --bg, 0 0 0 4px --focus-ring`).

### Named Rules

**The Flat-By-Default Rule.** Surfaces are flat at rest. Shadows are earned by hover, active state, or intentional structural promotion. The exception: `--glow-gold` on the primary CTA at rest is a warmth invitation, not an elevation signal.

**The Ink Ramp Rule.** Depth is primarily expressed through the ink ramp — `--ink-900` → `--ink-800` (bg) → `--ink-700` (surface) → `--ink-600` (card) → `--ink-500` (hover). Drop shadows reinforce this tonal layering; they never substitute for it.

## 5. Components

### Buttons

Buttons are inscribed, not filled — the label is always Cinzel tracked uppercase (13px, 0.18em), the shape near-sharp (2px radius). Hover states use a glow halo rather than a simple color shift; a 1px downward nudge on `:active` gives them weight.

- **Shape:** 2px / `--radius-sm`. No pills, no rounded-lg.
- **Duo (signature):** `--duo-sheen` gradient fill (greenery⟷bronze⟷greenery), `--ink-900` text. Reserved for the primary CTA. Hover: `brightness(1.08)` + `--glow-gold`.
- **Green:** Solid `--green-400` fill, `--parchment-100` text. Secondary CTA. Hover: `brightness(1.14)` + green halo.
- **Bronze:** Solid `--gold-500` fill, `--ink-900` text. For metallic-accent contexts only. Hover: `brightness(1.1)` + bronze halo.
- **Outline:** Transparent bg, `--line` border (floral-white hairline, 16% opacity), `--green-300` text. Hover: border shifts to green-300, faint green fill.
- **Ghost:** Transparent bg, no border, `--green-300` text. Hover: text shifts, hairline border appears. Lowest-hierarchy action.
- **Focus (all variants):** `0 0 0 2px --bg, 0 0 0 4px --focus-ring` — 2px ink offset, 4px sage halo. Never browser default.
- **Disabled:** `opacity: 0.42`, `cursor: not-allowed`, no glow or hover effect.

### Inputs / Fields

Hairline-bordered fields on `--ink-700` ground, gilt focus glow. Placeholder in `--graphite-400` — not mistakable for content.

- **Style:** `--ink-700` bg, `--parchment-100` text, `1px --line` border, `--radius-sm`.
- **Placeholder:** `--graphite-400` (#67625A). WCAG AA at 18px on `--ink-700`; verify at smaller sizes.
- **Focus:** Border to `--line-strong` (30% floral-white), `0 0 0 3px rgba(177,138,68,0.16)` gilt glow.
- **Error:** `--critical` border + icon. No red background fills.

### Choices (Radio / Checkbox)

A distinctive input system: radios render as rotated squares (diamonds), not circles. Selected state shows a gilt-sheen fill inside the mark. Checkboxes use a gold `✓` on the same upright square.

- **Row container:** `9px 13px` padding, `--radius-sm`, `--line-soft` border, `rgba(0,0,0,0.18)` glaze.
- **Radio mark:** 16×16px rotated 45° (diamond). Selected: `--gilt-sheen` gradient fill inside.
- **Checkbox mark:** 16×16px upright. Selected: gold `✓` via border rotation.
- **Tone variants:** `data-tone="green"` selected rows get `--line-green` inset shadow; `data-tone="gold"` get `--line-gold`. Keeps RSVP accept/decline states visually distinct beyond color alone.

### Cards / Containers

Two flavors:

- **Dark card:** `--ink-600` bg, `1px --line-gold` border (42% bronze hairline), `--radius-sm`, `32px` padding. Hover: border to `--line-strong`, `--shadow-lg`, `translateY(-2px)`.
- **Parchment card:** `--parchment-100` bg, `1px rgba(71,89,63,0.2)` green hairline. Headings, links, and eyebrows inside restore to ink colors on the cream surface.
- **Frame (`.gg-frame`):** Double-rule angular container — outer `--line` at 1px, inner `--line-soft` inset 5px. Used for hero content blocks and the RSVP form.

### Navigation

Sticky translucent bar at `--nav-h: 76px`. Background: `rgba(12,14,12,0.82)` + `blur(10px)` — dark enough to read labels, transparent enough to feel like a continuation of the page. Nav labels: Cinzel at 13px (`--fs-eyebrow`), 0.18em tracking (`--ls-label`). Links draw a left-to-right gold underline on hover.

### Ornament Utilities (Signature)

The knotwork/Deco framing system is the visual signature. These utilities appear across every page section.

- **`.gg-eyebrow`:** Cinzel, 13px, 0.34em tracking, uppercase, `--green-300`. A deliberate, named brand system — the Deco kicker feels like an inscription, not an AI section-grammar reflex.
- **`.gg-rule`:** Full-width ornamental divider: greenery hairline extends left → 7×7px bronze rotated-square diamond → gold hairline extends right. The signature micro-ornament.
- **`.gg-frame`:** Double-rule angular container (outer 1px `--line`, inner 1px `--line-soft` inset 5px). Near-sharp corners; the Deco architecture is in the layered lines.
- **`.gg-gilt`:** Metallic foil text via `--gilt-sheen` + `background-clip: text`. Reserved for small labels, the "&" ornament, accent marks. Never applied to body paragraphs.
- **`.gg-duo`:** The couple's foil treatment — `--duo-sheen` (greenery⟷bronze⟷greenery) via `background-clip: text`. Used for the couple's names in the hero and on the primary CTA. The most distinctive visual element; its power depends on restraint.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:

- **Do** use eucalyptus green (`--green-300` through `--green-500`) as the single lead color. Greenery carries sections solo or paired with floral white on the dark ground.
- **Do** verify WCAG AA for every new color pairing — especially 13px tracked eyebrows and input placeholders — against their actual background before shipping.
- **Do** use knotwork for structural or symbolic work only. An ornament that isn't doing structural or symbolic work doesn't belong.
- **Do** keep corners near-sharp. Default corner radius is `--radius-md` (3px); most structure uses 0–2px. True pills (`--radius-pill`) are reserved for chip components only.
- **Do** write button labels in tracked Cinzel uppercase with `--ls-label` (0.18em). This is what makes CTAs feel ceremonial rather than clinical.
- **Do** include a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative (crossfade or instant) for every animation — reveals, cross-fades, and the 700ms veil all require one.
- **Do** use Cormorant Garamond italic — not bold — for emphasis in body copy.
- **Do** cap body line length at 65–75ch. Apply `text-wrap: balance` on h1–h3 and `text-wrap: pretty` on prose.
- **Do** use `data-lead="green"` / `data-lead="bronze"` on sections for a warm/cool rhythm; don't hardcode accent colors per-section.

### Don't:

- **Don't** make this look like a recognizable wedding template — Zola, The Knot, Squarespace-Wedding. The entire point of Gilt & Grove is to look unmistakably theirs, not generated from a theme picker.
- **Don't** use cursive scripts, handwritten fonts, or botanical illustration. No calligraphy headlines, floral borders, eucalyptus-sprig drawings, or watercolor washes. Romance is carried by knotwork, metal, and type — never by letterform affectation.
- **Don't** use bronze/gold as a dominant surface fill, full button background, or section background. Bronze is the metallic tie. Its rarity is what makes it feel precious.
- **Don't** use bright/emerald green, bluish-purple gradients, neon, pure white (#FFFFFF), pastels, or large flat gold fills.
- **Don't** create a cold, corporate, or SaaS-landing feel — no harsh whitespace, businesslike neutrality, or clinical sterility.
- **Don't** let the logistics (date, place, dress code, RSVP) become hard to find. Grandeur and legibility must coexist; the guest's answer is always the fastest thing to find and read.
- **Don't** use drop shadows decoratively. Shadows and glow appear only as responses to state (hover, focus, promoted panels).
- **Don't** introduce a fourth typeface. The system has exactly three — Uncial Antiqua, Cinzel, Cormorant Garamond. A fourth breaks the Three-Face Pact immediately.
- **Don't** use `--critical` (#B4524A) for anything except form validation errors. It is the only color outside the brand palette and must remain isolated from brand contexts.
- **Don't** paste Celtic clip-art. Every knotwork element should be generated by the knot engine or be one of the approved SVG assets (`monogram.svg`, `sunburst.svg`, `sunburst-deco.svg`, `divider.svg`, `pattern-tile.svg`). Clip-art reads as costume.
